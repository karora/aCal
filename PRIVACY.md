
# Privacy Policy for aCal

**Effective Date:** April 20, 2026

Andrew McMillan ("I", "me", "my") built the **aCal** app as an Open Source / custom-built application. This Privacy Policy informs you of my policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information when you use aCal.

My core privacy principle is simple: **Your data is your own.** aCal is designed to connect your Android device directly to your chosen CalDAV servers. I do not collect, monitor, or have access to any of your personal data.

## Policy Details

### 1. Information Collection and Data Flow

**Direct Connection:** aCal operates entirely on your device. It communicates directly with the CalDAV server(s) you configure. Your data does not pass through any servers owned, operated, or controlled by me.

**No Developer Data Collection:** I do not collect, transmit, or store your calendar events, contacts, tasks, server URLs, usernames, passwords, or any other personal information.

### 2. Android Permissions Request and Use

To function correctly as a calendar client, aCal requires access to certain sensitive permissions on your Android device. All data accessed via these permissions remains strictly local to your device and your configured CalDAV server.

* **Notifications:** Required to provide you with local alerts and reminders for your upcoming scheduled events.

#### Possible Additional Permissions

The following permissions are not required by aCal for normal operation, but some additional functionality may avail of these permissions in order to synchronise your CalDAV calendars with the normal calendaring and contacts databases on your device.

* **Calendar:** Required to read, display, and write events to your device's calendar.
* **Contacts:** Required to access your contacts locally, primarily to allow you to add attendees to your calendar events.

### 3. Account Credentials and Security

Your server login credentials (usernames and passwords) and account configurations are stored locally in a private, app-specific database on your Android device. They are not stored using the global Android Account Manager, and they are never transmitted anywhere except directly to your configured CalDAV server to authenticate your sessions.

### 4. Third-Party Services and Analytics

I wrote all the code for aCal. The app **does not** contain any third-party tracking, advertising, or analytics SDKs (such as Google Analytics, AdMob, or Facebook SDK).

**Crash Reporting:** If the app crashes, you may be prompted by the Android operating system to send a crash report via Google Play Services. This is a standard Android feature. If you have opted into this at the device level, I may receive anonymized crash data through the Google Play Console, which helps me identify and fix bugs. This data does not contain your personal calendar or contact information.

### 5. Data Retention and Deletion

Because I do not collect or store any of your personal data on my servers, there is no data for me to delete upon request. To remove all your aCal data, simply delete your configured accounts within the app or uninstall the aCal app from your device.

### 6. Children's Privacy

aCal is a general audience application. While it is completely safe for all ages to use as a personal calendar tool, I do not knowingly collect any personal identifiable information from anyone, including children under the age of 13.

### 7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

I may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in the app or legal requirements. I will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Effective Date" at the top.
